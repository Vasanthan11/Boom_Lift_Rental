Scissor lift applications

Where scissor lifts deliver the most value

Scissor lifts are the go-to choice when you need efficient, repeatable access directly above the working area, with minimal setup time and a strong, guarded platform.

Interior fit-out & finishing: Scissor lifts are widely used for ceiling work, MEP installations, ducting, false ceilings, sprinkler systems and final finishing in retail spaces, offices and malls.

Warehousing & logistics: In warehouses and distribution centres, scissor lifts support racking installation, high-bay lighting, fire systems, signage and regular maintenance of overhead services.

Industrial facilities: Factories, workshops and processing plants use scissor lifts for access to equipment platforms, pipe racks, cable trays and production-line infrastructure.

Event venues & public buildings: Sports halls, exhibition centres and auditoriums often rely on scissor lifts for rigging, lighting changes, banner installation and periodic inspection of overhead structures.

Common scissor lift configurations

  • Electric slab scissor lifts – compact, low-noise machines for indoor floors.
  • Diesel rough-terrain scissors – four-wheel drive units for uneven outdoor sites.
  • Wide-deck platforms – extra room for multiple technicians and material handling.
  • Extension decks – sliding platform sections for reaching over machinery or obstacles.
  • Narrow-aisle models – slim machines that can navigate tight corridors and racking rows.

What to look for when choosing a scissor lift

Working height & platform capacity

Match the working height to your task, allowing a margin for safe positioning. Consider how many people will be on the platform and the combined weight of tools and materials.

Indoor vs. outdoor use

Electric scissor lifts are ideal for flat, finished floors indoors, while diesel rough-terrain scissors handle outdoor slabs, compacted ground and mixed surfaces.

Aisle width and turning space

Check door widths, aisles, ramps and turns along the travel path to make sure the selected scissor lift can move safely to and from the work area.

Typical specification ranges available

  • Working height: Approx. 6–18 m, depending on model.
  • Platform capacity: From single-person units to high-capacity decks for multiple users.
  • Power source: Battery electric, hybrid or diesel rough-terrain.
  • Platform extensions: Manual or powered roll-out decks for extra reach.
  • Floor conditions: Non-marking tyres for finished floors; lug tyres for outdoor sites.

What details help size the right scissor lift?

Share your required working height, the number of people on the platform, estimated weight of materials and tools, whether you’re working indoors or outdoors, and a simple description of floor conditions and access route.

Do scissor lifts need level ground?

Yes – scissor lifts are designed to work on firm, level surfaces within the limits specified by the manufacturer. Always follow site rules and never operate on soft or severely uneven ground.

When should I choose a boom lift instead of a scissor lift?

Choose a scissor lift when you only need to go straight up with a wide platform. Switch to a boom lift when you must reach over machinery, roof edges, façades or other obstacles where horizontal outreach is essential.
